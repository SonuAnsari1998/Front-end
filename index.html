My Favourite Person

Birsa Munda

Birsa Munda was a courageous tribal leader, freedom fighter, and folk hero who played a significant role in India’s struggle against British colonial rule. He was born on November 15, 1875, in Ulihatu village, in present-day Jharkhand, into the Munda tribe, one of the prominent tribal communities of the region. From an early age, Birsa witnessed the exploitation, injustice, and oppression faced by the tribal people under British rule and local landlords. Disturbed by the harsh treatment of his people and the destruction of their traditional way of life, Birsa Munda emerged as a leader who inspired the tribal masses to stand up for their rights. He led the famous Ulgulan (meaning “The Great Tumult” or rebellion) against the British in the late 19th century. His movement aimed to establish Munda Raj and remove the British and the exploitative landlords who seized tribal lands and forced the people into bonded labor. Birsa Munda also worked towards social reform within the tribal society. He opposed the conversion of tribals to Christianity by missionaries and encouraged the revival of traditional tribal customs and the worship of their own deity. He promoted unity, self-respect, and a return to indigenous cultural values. Under his leadership, the tribal people resisted the unjust land laws and forest policies imposed by the British. Although the rebellion was eventually suppressed, Birsa’s efforts led to the passing of the Chotanagpur Tenancy Act, which protected tribal land rights. Birsa Munda died on June 9, 1900, at the young age of 25, in British custody. Despite his short life, his bravery and vision left an everlasting impact. He is remembered as a symbol of tribal pride and resistance. His legacy continues to inspire struggles for the rights and dignity of indigenous and marginalized communities in India.




Dr. B.R. Ambedkar

Dr. Bhimrao Ramji Ambedkar, popularly known as Dr. B.R. Ambedkar, was one of India’s greatest social reformers, jurists, economists, and political leaders. Born on April 14, 1891, in Mhow, Madhya Pradesh, into a Dalit (formerly “untouchable”) family, he faced severe social discrimination and exclusion from a young age. Despite these hardships, Ambedkar pursued education with extraordinary determination and became one of the first Dalits to obtain a college education in India. He later earned doctorates from Columbia University and the London School of Economics. Dr. Ambedkar dedicated his life to fighting social injustice, inequality, and caste discrimination. He strongly believed in the values of liberty, equality, and fraternity. His relentless efforts to secure rights for the marginalized communities played a key role in the upliftment of the Dalits and other oppressed sections of society. As the principal architect of the Indian Constitution, Dr. Ambedkar was instrumental in framing laws that guaranteed fundamental rights and abolished untouchability. His vision ensured that India would be a secular, democratic, and just society. He also championed women's rights, labor reforms, and the protection of minorities. Apart from his political achievements, Dr. Ambedkar was a profound scholar, writer, and thinker. His works, such as Annihilation of Caste, exposed the deep-rooted injustices within the Indian social structure. In 1956, towards the end of his life, he embraced Buddhism along with millions of followers, as a way to escape caste oppression. Dr. B.R. Ambedkar passed away on December 6, 1956. His legacy continues to inspire millions across India and the world. He is remembered not only as the “Father of the Indian Constitution” but also as a symbol of courage, resilience, and the fight for human dignity and social justice.




A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, widely known as the "Missile Man of India," was one of the most beloved and respected scientists and leaders in Indian history. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, into a humble family. Despite facing financial hardships, his determination and thirst for knowledge led him to pursue a degree in aerospace engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology. Dr. Kalam began his career at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and later played a key role in India’s space and missile programs at the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). He was instrumental in the development of India's first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III), which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in orbit in 1980. His work on the development of ballistic missiles like Agni and Prithvi earned him the nickname "Missile Man." One of his most significant contributions came during India’s nuclear tests at Pokhran in 1998, which established India as a nuclear power. Despite his scientific achievements, Dr. Kalam remained a humble and spiritual man, deeply connected to the common people, especially the youth. He strongly believed that education, innovation, and hard work were the keys to personal and national development. In 2002, Dr. Kalam became the 11th President of India, serving from 2002 to 2007. Known as the "People's President," he was admired for his simplicity, integrity, and visionary ideas. Even after his presidency, he continued to inspire young minds through his books, lectures, and interactions. His famous books like Wings of Fire, Ignited Minds, and India 2020 continue to motivate millions to dream big and work towards the betterment of society. Dr. Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management, Shillong—doing what he loved most: teaching and inspiring the youth. His life is a shining example of how dedication, humility, and vision can transform not only an individual’s destiny but also that of an entire nation. He remains an enduring symbol of hope, hard work, and patriotism.




Mahatma Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi, whose full name was Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi, is one of the most iconic leaders in the history of India and the world. He was born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, Gujarat. Gandhi is best known for his philosophy of non-violence, known as Ahimsa, and his method of peaceful resistance, called Satyagraha. After completing his education in law in London, Gandhi worked in South Africa, where he first experienced racial discrimination. It was there that he began his fight against injustice using non-violent means. Upon returning to India in 1915, he became the leader of the Indian freedom movement against British colonial rule. Gandhi inspired millions of Indians to join the struggle for independence through peaceful protests, civil disobedience, and non-cooperation with the British government. His famous movements include the Salt March of 1930 and the Quit India Movement of 1942. Through his actions, he demonstrated that freedom could be achieved not through violence but through unity, truth, and non-violence. Beyond politics, Gandhi emphasized simple living, self-reliance, and the importance of moral values. He promoted the use of khadi (hand-spun cloth) to support rural artisans and reduce dependence on British goods. His teachings on tolerance, equality, and respect for all religions left a deep impact on Indian society. Mahatma Gandhi’s leadership not only helped India gain independence on August 15, 1947, but also inspired civil rights movements across the world, including those led by Martin Luther King Jr. and Nelson Mandela. Tragically, Gandhi was assassinated on January 30, 1948, but his message of peace, truth, and non-violence continues to guide people worldwide. He is fondly remembered as the “Father of the Nation” and a symbol of justice and humanity. Dr. Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management, Shillong—doing what he loved most: teaching and inspiring the youth. His life is a shining example of how dedication, humility, and vision can transform not only an individual’s destiny but also that of an entire nation. He remains an enduring symbol of hope, hard work, and patriotism.




Ratan Tata

Ratan Tata is one of India’s most respected industrialists, philanthropists, and visionary leaders. He was born on December 28, 1937, in Mumbai, into the prominent Tata family, which has been at the forefront of India's industrial growth for over a century. He studied architecture and structural engineering at Cornell University and completed an advanced management program at Harvard Business School. Ratan Tata joined the Tata Group in 1962 and worked in various companies within the group before becoming the Chairman of Tata Sons in 1991. Under his leadership, the Tata Group transformed into a global business giant, with successful ventures in steel, automobiles, IT, hospitality, and more. He played a key role in the launch of the Tata Indica, India’s first indigenously developed car, and later the Tata Nano, which was the world's cheapest car aimed at making automobile ownership accessible to the common man. One of Ratan Tata’s most notable achievements was the acquisition of prestigious global brands such as Tetley Tea, Jaguar-Land Rover, and Corus Steel, which expanded the global presence of Indian industry. His leadership style, marked by humility, integrity, and vision, earned him immense respect both in India and internationally. Apart from his business success, Ratan Tata is widely admired for his philanthropic work. Through the Tata Trusts, he has supported numerous initiatives in education, healthcare, rural development, and social welfare. His commitment to ethical business practices and social responsibility has set a high standard for corporate India. Ratan Tata stepped down as chairman in 2012 but remains an influential figure and a guiding force in the Tata Group. He has received several national and international honors, including the Padma Bhushan and Padma Vibhushan. He is not just a business icon but a role model for leadership, compassion, and service to society.



Mahendra Singh Dhoni

Mahendra Singh Dhoni, popularly known as MS Dhoni, is one of India’s most successful and beloved cricketers. He was born on July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, Jharkhand. Coming from a humble background, Dhoni’s journey to the top of international cricket is a story of hard work, determination, and extraordinary talent. Dhoni made his debut for the Indian cricket team in 2004 and quickly gained attention for his aggressive batting style and sharp wicket-keeping skills. However, it was his calm temperament, sharp decision-making, and leadership qualities that truly set him apart. In 2007, Dhoni was appointed captain of the Indian T20 team and led India to victory in the inaugural ICC T20 World Cup, instantly winning the hearts of millions. As the captain of the Indian team across all formats, Dhoni achieved remarkable success. Under his leadership, India won the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup, the 2013 ICC Champions Trophy, and reached the top position in Test cricket rankings. He is the only captain in the history of cricket to have won all three major ICC trophies. His famous winning six in the 2011 World Cup final remains one of the most iconic moments in Indian sports history. Known as "Captain Cool," Dhoni is admired for his ability to stay composed under pressure and for making bold and smart decisions on the field. Apart from his leadership, he is regarded as one of the best finishers in limited-overs cricket. Off the field, Dhoni is known for his humility, simplicity, and dedication. He has also served as a Lieutenant Colonel in the Indian Territorial Army, reflecting his respect for the armed forces. MS Dhoni retired from international cricket in 2020 but continues to play in the Indian Premier League (IPL), leading Chennai Super Kings (CSK) to multiple titles. His legacy as a cricketing legend and a national icon continues to inspire millions.
